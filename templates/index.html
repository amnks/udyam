ABOUT US

UDYAM is the Annual Technical Festival of the Department of Electronics Engineering, IIT (BHU) Varanasi. UDYAM offers an opportunity for students to treat themselves to a technical extravaganza. It gives you a chance to be more actively involved in the learning experience at the heart of effective technology integration. It is a means for students to showcase their practical talent in their learning and not just limit themselves to theoretical knowledge. With this idea of an application-oriented approach, UDYAM started in 2010 with a vision of providing a platform for the community of Indian students to develop and showcase their technical prowess. It is not just these head to head competitions that you can get at Udyam. It's about the events that make you proficient with the industry standards. The limelight is on you.

For the last 11 years, UDYAM has grown exponentially, crossing all boundaries. This year marks the 12'th Edition of UDYAM, IIT (BHU) Varanasi. Concentrating on a vast number of exciting and engaging events, this edition of UDYAM promises to be elevated, unpredicted, and more prominent than ever before.

Welcome to UDYAM'21.

I-CHIP

I-Chip is a Verilog-based event where participants learn to design, simulate, validate, and debug digital systems ranging from simple flip-flops to complex microprocessors using Hardware Description Language. The event allows learning Verilog HDL and working with FPGA Boards. The participants are taught the Vivado simulator's usage for behavioral and timing simulations of these systems, a crucial asset for any Hardware Design & Verification Engineer.

Commnet is a Digital Communication based event focusing on the various techniques and methodologies used in Digital Communication Systems such as Coding Theory, Compression Algos, Signal Processing, Modulation & Demodulation techniques, etc. Participants get to learn these techniques and implement them using MATLAB.

Continuum is an Analog Electronics based event where the participants learn to design and simulate analog circuits & filters to perform computations to solve the given problem statement. Participants learn to use CAD tools for designing and simulating circuits and also get an opportunity to implement and validate their designs on hardware.
